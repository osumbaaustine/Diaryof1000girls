Sayonara

“Sometimes I’ll start a sentence and I don’t even know where it’s going. I just hope I find it along the way.” Michael Scott, The Office USA.

To some extent, that is the story of Diaryof1000girls blog. This blog started out as an outlet for a teenage girl trying understand her place and purpose. I wasn’t sure what it was going to be all I knew was that this was an important journey I needed to take. After a while, Diaryof1000 became a safe space that taught me to be brave and allowed me to understand my identities and those of others.

To my surprise, Diaryoof1000girls found a life of its own and grew into a community of sharing , learning, and unlearning different perspectives and perceptions about each other. It was a constantly evolving journey that explored different issues from mental health, sexuality, reproductive health rights to trying to understand emotions and identities. Through shared curiosity and solidarity in our humanity we sparked conversations and created something truly special.

As someone with multiple intersecting identities I often felt lonely, confused, and invisible. My identities felt like a burden as I found myself facing discrimination and inequalities from almost every aspect of my life. However, the more stories and conversations I had through Diaryof1000girls the better I understood my reality and found comfort in knowing that I was never alone. I got a better understanding of the pain and danger of being unheard, misunderstood, and unrepresented. I also found strength in knowing that there are people who are not comfortable with the status quo of discrimination and seek to create a better world.

In one way or another through your support we have amplified each other’s voices, created a community , and support system to navigate our lived realities. We have shared our lessons, journeys laugher, frustrations and tears and become better people for it.

I know I have definitely become a better person and beyond that, through this space I was able to understand my purpose and mission. I have seen the power of storytelling as I shared my own stories and those of others and this has fueled me to pursue advocacy passionately and relentlessly. I very clearly understand that my purpose is to be part of those ‘better world seekers’.

In my capacity I want to work towards amplifying people’s voices, especially women, who for so long have been silenced or made to feel small. People whose realities are constantly misinterpreted, ignored, and erased. I want our truths to be known and to work towards getting actions done to improve our realities.

Understanding this mission also came with the bittersweet truth that I need to take a different approach to achieve this goal. At the moment this means saying goodbye to Diaryof1000girls blog while I go off to do some soul searching and researching. The WordPress blog might be done but the mission is still very much alive and the content will still be available.

That being said, I am looking forward to finding new ways to amplify the voices of those who feel unheard, sharing my experiences and working towards building a responsive world that upholds every persons humanity and dignity above all other identities.

I’m specifically grateful to those of you who trusted me with your truths and vulnerabilities by sharing your stories with me and allowing me to share them with the world. It is your voices that have kept this space going for almost five years. Heartfelt gratitude to my team of unpaid editors and content curation team who have been the spine of my creative process. I continue to rely on your love, feedback and support.
